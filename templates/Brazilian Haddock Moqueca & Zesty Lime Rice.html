Brazilian Haddock Moqueca & Zesty Lime Rice

★★★★

Brazilian, Dinner, Fish

Total Time: 20 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 red pepper

1 lime

1 garlic clove

1 spring onion

1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes

200g haddock (works well with frozen fish pie chunks)

5g coriander

130g basmati rice

1 vegetable stock cube

200ml coconut milk

2 tsp chipotle paste

Description

Taste the sunshine in this South American fish stew known as Moqueca (mo-keh-kah). We’ve packed it with haddock, sweet red pepper, coconut and lime for mouthwatering zestiness. Delicioso!

Directions

1) Zest 1/2 [1] lime

2) Roll the lime[s] with your hand on a hard surface (to release more juice!) and cut in half

3) Add the lime zest, basmati rice and 300ml [600ml] cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat

4) Squeeze in the juice of 1/2 [1] lime and add the squeezed lime half to the pot

5) Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has absorbed and the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving – this is your zesty lime rice

6) While the rice is cooking, boil a kettle

7) Cut the haddock into bite sized pieces

8) Trim, then slice the spring onion[s] finely

9) Peel and finely chop the garlic

10) Deseed the red pepper and cut into thin strips

11) Heat a large, wide-based pan with a matching lid, with a drizzle of olive oil over a high heat. Once hot, add the sliced red pepper and cook for 3 min or until it begins to soften

12) Add the chopped garlic, sliced spring onion and chilli flakes

13) Reduce the heat to medium and cook for 3 min

14) Meanwhile, remove the coconut cream from the sachet[s] and chop it roughly. Dissolve the chopped coconut cream, vegetable stock cube, chipotle paste, 1 tsp [2 tsp] sugar, and the juice of the remaining lime in 300ml [600ml] boiled water – this is your coconut stock

15) Strip the coriander leaves from their stalks and chop the stalks finely (save the leaves for garnish!)

16) Add the chopped coriander stalks to the pan and cook for 1 min

17) Add the coconut stock cook for 2-3 min or until the sauce has slightly thickened

18) Once the sauce has thickened, add the haddock and cook, covered, for 3-4 min or until the fish is cooked through – this is your Brazilian haddock moqueca

19) Once cooked, remove the squeezed lime half from the cooked rice and fluff it with a fork

20) Serve the Brazilian haddock moqueca with the zesty lime rice and garnish with the coriander leaves
