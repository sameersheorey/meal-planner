Pear and Frangipane Tart

Pies

Prep Time: 1 hr Cook Time: 30-40 mins Servings: 8 Source: BakedIn Box

Ingredients

45ml cold water

170g unsalted butter (110g frozen, 60g softened)

225g flour (215g + 10g + plus extra for dusting)

1/4 tsp salt

120g caster sugar

60g ground almonds

2 medium eggs

2 large ripe pears (~275g unpeeled)

1/16 tsp Madagascan vanilla

5g icing sugar

Directions

1) Measure out 45ml of cold water

2) Coarsely grate the frozen butter (110g) into a large bowl and rub in 215g of flour. Add the water a tablespoon at a time until the pastry comes together.

3) Turn onto a clean surface and knead lightly until a dough forms. Flatten into a ~15cm disc, wrap and chill for 20 mins in fridge (10 mins in freezer)

4) Whilst the pastry is chilling make the frangipane. Beat together the softened butter (60g) and caster sugar (60g) until light and fluffy. Beat in 1 egg and when fully incorporated, add the ground almonds and stir until combined. Chill until needed.

5) Peel the pairs and cut into quarters lengthwise. Remove the core from each quarter and slice lengthwise into 3/4 pieces. Toss the slices with caster sugar (60g), flour (10g) and vanilla until coated.

6) Preheat oven to 180 C fan/gas mark 6

7) Prepare 18cm round tin by greasing, crossing with two long rectangular strips of baking paper and covering with round of baking paper (see picture).

8) To prepare tart case, cut of 1/3 of pastry and return to fridge. Lightly dust surface with flour and roll out the remaining 2/3 of pastry to 3mm thickness and slightly larger than the tin.

9) Transfer the pastry to the tin and gently press it into the base (ensuring the baking paper strips remain visible). Prick the base all over with a fork and trim case to leave a 1cm overhang around the edge.

10) Stir the frangipane and spoon into the pastry case spread to the edges with the back of a spoon.

11) Leaving any excess liquid in the bowl lay the pear slices in a spiral on top of the frangipane, working from the edge to the middle.

12) To make the lattice lightly dust the surface with more flour. Take the remaining pastry from the fridge and roll and trim to a 20cm by 20 cm square. and cut into 10 2x20cm strips.

13) Evenly lay 5 strips parallel across the tart, fold back every other strip and place one long strip perpendicular down the centre of the tart. Unfold the strips across this new strip.

14) Fold back the strips running the new strip and lay down a second strip and unfold across the new strip. Repeat until all 5 perpendicular strips are laid.

15) Use a fork to press down firmly around the edges to form a bond between the lattice and the tart. Trim the edges around the tin.

16) Crack an egg into a small bowl and whisk with a fork. Lightly egg wash the top of the tart and bake for 30-40 minutes until golden brown on the top.

17) Leave to cool in tin for 10 minutes then lift onto cooling rack using baking paper strips. Dust with icing sugar.

Notes

Can be eaten warm or cold with ice cream or custard.
