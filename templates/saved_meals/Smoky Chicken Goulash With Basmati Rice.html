Smoky Chicken Goulash With Basmati Rice

Chicken, Hungarian

Total Time: 25 Servings: 2 Source: Gousto

Ingredients

1 brown onion

1 tsp smoked paprika

1 romano pepper

5g fresh parsley

100g basmati rice

2 tsp ground paprika

1 chicken stock cube

80g chestnut mushrooms

32g tomato paste

100g soured cream

2 x 125g chicken breast fillets

Description

For this twist on a popular Hungarian stew, you'll simmer tender chicken breast in a smoky tomato sauce with pepper and mushrooms. Serve it with rice, fresh parsley and sour cream. Gorgeous!

Directions

1) Boil a kettle

2) Heat a very large, wide-based pan with a lid, with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the chicken breasts and cook for 5 min or until golden, turning once halfway

3) Meanwhile, peel and slice the brown onion finely and deseed the Romano pepper and cut into thin strips

4) Push the chicken breasts to one side of the pan and reduce the heat to medium-low. Add the sliced onion and Romano pepper with a generous pinch of salt and cook, covered, for 4-5 min or until beginning to soften

5) Meanwhile, chop the chestnut mushrooms into quarters

6) Dissolve the chicken stock cube, tomato paste and 1 tsp sugar in 350ml [650ml] boiled water – this is your tomato stock

7) Add the basmati rice and 250ml cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has absorbed and the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving

8) Once the vegetables are beginning to soften, add the chopped mushrooms, ground paprika and smoked paprika to the pan and cook for 1-2 min or until fragrant

9) Add the tomato stock, bring to the boil over a high heat and cook, covered, for 5 min further or until the chicken is cooked through

10) Meanwhile, chop the parsley roughly, including the stalks

11) Once cooked, transfer the chicken to a clean board and shred it apart, using two forks. Return the shredded chicken to the pan and season generously with salt and pepper. Give everything a good mix up – this is your smoky chicken goulash

12) Serve the smoky chicken goulash in bowls with the basmati rice to the side. Top with a dollop of soured cream, a good grind of black pepper and the chopped parsley
