Creamy Smoked Salmon and Asparagus Linguini

Fish, Italian

Prep Time: 10 mins Cook Time: 40 mins Total Time: 50 min Servings: 4 Source: Killingthyme.net

Ingredients

0.5 tbsp olive oil

2 cloves of garlic (minced)

1 diced red onion

75g cherry tomatoes

180g roughly chopped asparagus

1 vegetable stock cube

150g linguini

125g mushrooms

1 lemon

120ml milk

40g cream cheese

120g smoked salmon, roughly chopped

Directions

1) Heat the olive oil in a large pan with a lid over medium heat.

2) Add the garlic, onions, tomatoes and asparagus and sauté for about 5-7 minutes until tender. Set aside for later.

3) Dissolve stock cube in 500ml water

4) Add the vegetable stock to the pan and bring to a simmer. Scrape any browned bits to deglaze the pan.

5) Add the pasta and mushrooms into the simmering broth, and lower the heat to medium-low. Add the fresh squeezed lemon juice.

6) Let the pasta simmer until its al dente, this should take about 25 minutes. Add extra water as needed.

7) When the pasta is nearly done, add the milk and the cream cheese, as well as a pinch of salt and pepper. Stir well to mix and melt the cheese, and keep tossing the pasta until completely coated.

8) Once the sauce has thickened and coated the pasta, add the cooked onion/garlic/asparagus you set aside earlier as well as the lemon zest, and smoked salmon. Toss again to distribute the ingredients throughout the pasta.

Notes

With asparagus, no one knows exactly where the "woody" end starts and finishes. The best way to figure it out is to hold an asparagus spear between your fingers and snap it in half, like a twig. The asparagus spear will break exactly where it needs to, closer to the end of the spear.
