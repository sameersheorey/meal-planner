Smoked Fishcakes With Minty Pea & Rocket Salad

British, Dinner, Fish

Total Time: 45 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

3 spring onions

5g mint

200g smoked basa

1 tbsp white wine vinegar

20g rocket

30g panko breadcrumbs

4 white potatoes

2 tbsp mayonnaise

160g blanched peas

Description

These flavourful fishcakes are packed with smoked fish and zingy spring onion, then coated in breadcrumbs for amazing crunch. Serve with minty mayonnaise and a crisp pea and rocket salad. Gorgeous!

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 200°C (fan)/Gas 7

2) Boil a kettle

3) Cut the potatoes (skins on) into large bite-sized pieces

4) Add the chopped potatoes to a pot of plenty of boiled water with a pinch of salt and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, cook for 12-15 min or until fork-tender, then drain and return to the pot

5) Meanwhile, add the smoked basa fillets to a baking tray with a drizzle of olive oil and a grind of black pepper. Put the tray in the oven for 12-15 min or until the fish is cooked through. Once cooked, flake the fish roughly with a fork and reserve the tray

6) Meanwhile, slice the spring onions finely

7) Strip the mint leaves from their stems and chop them roughly, discard the stems

8) Combine the mayonnaise with half the chopped mint, half the white wine vinegar, 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper – this is your fresh mint mayo

9) Re-boil a kettle

10) Run the drained potatoes under the cold tap until they're cool enough to handle, then return them to the pot, add a knob of butter and crush gently

11) Add the flaked fish and sliced spring onion, season with a generous pinch of salt and pepper and mix gently

12) Divide the mix into 4 [8], then shape them into fish cakes

13) Press the fishcakes into the panko breadcrumbs until evenly coated

14) Add the fishcakes to the reserved tray with a drizzle of olive oil. Put the tray in the oven for 15-20 min or until golden and crispy

15) Whilst the fishcakes are cooking, add the peas to a pot of boiled water and cook for 2-3 min or until tender

16) Combine the remaining white wine vinegar with 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper – this is your dressing

17) Add the drained peas, remaining mint, rocket and dressing to a bowl and give everything a good mix up – this is your minty pea & rocket salad

18) Serve the fishcakes with the minty pea & rocket salad to the side. Drizzle the fresh mint mayo over the fishcakes
