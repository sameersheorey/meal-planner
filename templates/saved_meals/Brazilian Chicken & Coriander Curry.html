Brazilian Chicken & Coriander Curry

Brazilian, Chicken, Dinner

Prep Time: 30 min Total Time: 30 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 brown onion

130g brown rice

1 lime

1 garlic clove

15g fresh root ginger

1 tsp coriander seeds

10g coriander

125g cherry tomatoes

1 large chicken breast fillet

1 tsp ground turmeric

1/2 tsp cayenne pepper

1 chicken stock cube

1 tbsp smooth peanut butter

50g solid coconut cream

Description

Fresh coriander and lime give this chicken curry a mouthwatering zing. You'll stir up a rich coconut, cherry tomato and peanut butter sauce, and serve it over wholesome brown rice. Delicioso!

Directions

1) Boil a kettle

2) Rinse the brown rice, add it to a pot with plenty of cold water and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once the rice is boiling, reduce the heat to medium and cook for 15-20 min or until it's tender with a slight bite. Once cooked, drain and return it to the pot and keep covered until serving

3) While the rice is cooking, heat a large, wide-based pan over a medium-high heat. Once hot, add the coriander seeds and cook for 1 min or until fragrant

4) Once fragrant, transfer the coriander seeds to a pestle & mortar and crush roughly

5) Cut the chicken breast into small bite-sized pieces. Return the pan to a high heat with a drizzle of vegetable oil. Once hot, add the diced chicken with a pinch of salt and cook for 6-7 min or until browned

6) While the chicken is browning, peel and finely slice the brown onion.

7) Once browned, transfer the browned chicken to a plate, reduce the heat to medium-low and add the sliced onion to the pan. Cook for 4-5 min or until softened

8) Peel and finely chop the garlic and ginger

9) Dissolve the chicken stock cube, peanut butter and coconut cream in 350ml [650ml] boiled water – this is your coconut stock

10) Once the onion has softened, add the chopped garlic, ginger, ground turmeric, cayenne pepper and crushed coriander seeds to the onion and cook for 1-2 min or until fragrant

11) Increase the heat to medium, add the cherry tomatoes and coconut stock and cook for 7-9 min or until thickened

12) Meanwhile, chop the coriander finely, including the stalks (reserve some leaves for garnish!)

13) Add the browned chicken and chopped coriander to the sauce and cook for 1 min further – this is your Brazilian chicken & coriander curry

14) Slice the lime into wedges

15) Add the juice of half the lime wedges to the curry (save the rest for garnish!)

16) Serve the Brazilian chicken & coriander curry over the cooked brown rice. Garnish with the reserved coriander leaves and lime wedges
