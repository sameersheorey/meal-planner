Nutty Bang Bang Chicken & Rice Salad

Chicken, Chinese, Dinner

Total Time: 25 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 red chilli

1 little gem lettuce

3 tbsp soy sauce

2 spring onions

1/2 cucumber

1 large chicken breast fillet

100g basmati rice

1/2 tsp cayenne pepper

1/2 tsp cinnamon

1 carrot

1 tbsp toasted sesame oil

1 tbsp rice vinegar

1 tbsp smooth peanut butter

20g toasted sesame seeds

Description

For an easy twist on a Chinese ‘Bang Bang’ recipe, you'll pull tender British chicken breast so it's extra juicy. Serve with a nutty, umami-rich peanut and soy dressing. Delicious!

Directions

1) Boil a kettle

2) Add the basmati rice and 250ml [500ml] cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-15 min or until all the water has been absorbed and the rice is cooked, then remove from the heat and keep covered until serving

3) Meanwhile, heat a large, wide-based pan with a matching lid, with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium-high heat. Once hot, add the chicken breast with a generous pinch of both salt and pepper and cook for 3 min on each side, or until golden

4) Shred the little gem finely

5) Top, tail, peel, then grate the carrot

6) Trim, then slice the spring onions finely

7) Add the shredded little gem, grated carrot and sliced spring onion to a large mixing bowl

8) Once browned, reduce the heat to medium-low and sprinkle the cayenne and cinnamon over the chicken. Turn the chicken breast several times until evenly coated in the spice.

9) Cook for 1 min further, then add a splash of water and cook, covered, for 8-12 min or until the chicken is cooked through

10) While the chicken is cooking, cut the cucumber into discs. Stack a few cucumber discs at a time and cut each stack into matchsticks

11) Cut half of the red chilli into rounds

12) Add the cucumber matchsticks and chilli rounds to the bowl with the little gem, carrot and spring onion – this is your crunchy salad

13) Cut the remaining chilli in half lengthways, deseed and chop finely

14) Combine the peanut butter, soy sauce, toasted sesame oil, rice vinegar and chopped chilli in a small bowl

15) Add 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] boiled water with 1 tsp [2 tsp] sugar and mix to combine – this is your peanut dressing

16) Transfer the cooked chicken to a chopping board and shred it using two forks. Once shredded, season the chicken with a pinch of salt – this is your bang bang chicken

17) Fluff the cooked rice with a fork and add it to the crunchy salad. Dress the crunchy salad with the peanut dressing and a drizzle of olive oil and season with a generous pinch of salt

18) Serve the bang bang chicken over the crunchy salad. Garnish with a sprinkling of sesame seeds
