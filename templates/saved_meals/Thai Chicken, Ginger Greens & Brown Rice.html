Thai Chicken, Ginger Greens & Brown Rice

Chicken, Dinner, Thai

Prep Time: 25 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 shallot

1 red chilli

1 red pepper

1 lime

1 garlic clove

15g fresh root ginger

10g coriander

150g spring greens

80g sugar snap peas

100g brown rice

1 tbsp soy sauce

25g solid coconut cream

250g chicken breast fillets

Description

Think making healthy choices means being hungry? Think again! This Thai-style dish combines protein-packed chicken breast, fibre-rich brown rice and vibrant veg to keep you fuller for longer.

Directions

1) Rinse the brown rice, add it to a pot with plenty of cold water and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium and cook for 15-20 min or until it's tender with a slight bite. Once cooked, drain, return it to the pot and keep covered until serving

2) Boil half a kettle

3) Peel and roughly chop the shallot and garlic

4) Cut the red chilli in half lengthways, deseed and chop finely

5) Chop the lime in half

6) Chop the coriander finely, including the stalks, keeping the leaves and stalks separate

7) Add the chopped shallot, garlic, chilli and coriander stalks to a food processor. Add a pinch of salt and grind to a paste – this is your Thai paste

8) Dissolve the coconut cream in 150ml [250ml] boiled water and add the soy sauce – this is your coconut stock

9) Heat a large, wide-based pan with 1/2 tbsp [1 tbsp] vegetable oil over a medium-high heat. Once hot, add the chicken breasts and cook for 3 min on each side or until browned

10) Once browned, add the Thai paste to the chicken and cook for 2-3 min further or until fragrant

11) Once fragrant, reduce the heat to medium, add the coconut stock and cook for 10-12 min or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened

12) Add the juice of 1/2 [1] lime and stir it all together – this is your Thai sauce

13) Cut the remaining lime into wedges

14) Meanwhile, rip the leaves off the spring greens, discard the tough stalks, then layer the leaves over each other, roll them up and slice into thin strips

15) Deseed the red pepper and cut into thin strips. Slice the sugar snap peas in half lengthways. Peel and finely chop the ginger

16) Heat a separate, large, wide-based pan over a high heat with 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] vegetable oil. Once hot, add the chopped red pepper, sliced spring greens and sugar snap peas with a pinch of salt and cook for 4-6 min or until starting to soften

17) Add the chopped ginger and cook for a further 1-2 min or until everything is cooked with a slight bite – these are your ginger greens

18) Slice the cooked chicken finely and serve with the brown rice and ginger greens to the side. Drizzle the Thai sauce all over. Garnish with the chopped coriander leaves and a lime wedge
