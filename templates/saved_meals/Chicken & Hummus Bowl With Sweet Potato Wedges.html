Chicken & Hummus Bowl With Sweet Potato Wedges

Chicken, Dinner, Middle Eastern

Prep Time: 30 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 lemon

1 red onion

2 garlic cloves

1 spring onion

1 large chicken breast fillet

1 tsp smoked paprika

1 tsp dried chilli flakes

2 tbsp ras el hanout

1 can of chickpeas

40g tahini

1/2 tbsp tomato paste

400g white flesh sweet potatoes

Description

This deliciously satisfying bowl bridges the gap between wholesome and comforting. You'll make a simple homemade hummus, which acts as your creamy base. You'll then top it with succulent, smoky spiced chicken and serve with oven-baked sweet potato wedges. Sweet! (Gluten and dairy-free, suitable for coeliacs.)

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 180°C (fan)/Gas 7

2) Cut the sweet potatoes (skins on) into wedges. Add the wedges to a large baking tray with a drizzle of vegetable oil and a generous pinch of salt and give everything a good mix up. Put the tray in the oven for 20 min or until the wedges are tender and starting to crisp

3) While the wedges are cooking, drain and rinse the chickpeas and add them to a food processor

4) Juice the lemon into the processor

5) Peel the garlic and chop roughly 

6) Add the garlic, tahini, 50ml [100ml] olive oil, a splash of water and 1 tsp [2 tsp] salt to the processor. Blitz until smooth and creamy then set aside – this is your hummus 


7) Chop the chicken breast into bite-sized pieces. Add the diced chicken to a mixing bowl with the ras el hanout, chilli flakes and most of the smoked paprika (save a pinch for garnish!). Season with a generous pinch of salt and mix to combine the chicken in the spices

8) Peel and finely slice the red onion

9) Heat a large, wide-based pan with 2 tbsp [4 tbsp] vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the sliced onion and cook for 3-4 min 

10) Add the spiced chicken and cook for 2-4 min further until the chicken is browned

11) Once the chicken is browned, add the tomato paste, 1/2 tsp [1 tsp] sugar and 100ml [200ml] water to the pan. Cook for 2-4 min further or until the chicken is cooked through and a sauce has formed. This is your spicy chicken

12) Trim and slice the spring onion[s] finely

13) Serve the spicy chicken over the hummus with the sweet potato wedges to the side
