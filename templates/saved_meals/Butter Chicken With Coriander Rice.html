Butter Chicken With Coriander Rice

Chicken, Dinner, Indian

Total Time: 35 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 brown onion

2 garlic cloves

15g fresh root ginger

10g coriander

40g Cornish clotted cream

1 tsp ground turmeric

1 tsp ground coriander

1 tbsp garam masala

130g basmati rice

1/2 chicken stock cube

1 tbsp tomato paste

250g chicken breast fillets

Description

Butter chicken or 'murgh makhani' is a mildly spiced curry house favourite. To make your own, you'll simmer British chicken breast in a silky tomato sauce until tender, and serve with coriander rice.

Directions

1) Add the basmati rice and 300ml [600ml] cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has absorbed and the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving

2) Cut the chicken breasts into large, bite-sized pieces. Combine the chopped chicken with the ground coriander, 1 tsp [2 tsp] of the garam masala and a generous pinch of salt

3) Heat a large, wide-based pan with a matching lid, with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a high heat. Once hot, add the chopped chicken and cook for 4 min, turning occasionally until the chicken is browned. Once browned, transfer the chicken to a plate and reserve the pan

4) Boil half a kettle

5) Peel and finely dice the brown onion

6) Return the reserved pan to a medium heat with 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] butter

7) Once melted, add the diced onion with a pinch of salt and cook for 5 min or until softened and starting to caramelise

8) Peel and finely chop the ginger and garlic

9) Once softened, add the chopped ginger, chopped garlic, ground turmeric, tomato paste and remaining garam masala to the pan and cook for 1 min or until fragrant

10) Crumble in 1/2 [1] chicken stock cube, then add 250ml [450ml] boiled water

11) Return the chicken to the pan and cook, covered, for 4-5 min or until the sauce has thickened to a curry-like consistency and the chicken is cooked through

12) Meanwhile, chop the coriander finely, including the stalks (save some leaves for garnish!)

13) Fluff the cooked rice with a fork, stir through the chopped coriander and season with salt – this is your coriander rice

14) Once the chicken is cooked, remove the pan from the heat and stir in the clotted cream – this is your butter chicken

15) Serve the butter chicken over the coriander rice. Garnish with the reserved coriander leaves
