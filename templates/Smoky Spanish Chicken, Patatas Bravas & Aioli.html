Smoky Spanish Chicken, Patatas Bravas & Aioli

Chicken, Dinner, Spanish

Prep Time: 30 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 red onion

1 red pepper

1 tomato

1 yellow pepper

1 garlic clove

2 tsp smoked paprika

2 tsp ground coriander

1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes

5g parsley

1 tbsp tomato paste

4 white potatoes

3 tbsp mayonnaise

250g chicken breast fillets

Description

In this twist on a tapas classic, you'll serve patatas bravas (crispy potatoes topped with a spicy tomato sauce) with smoky British chicken breast and a dollop of garlicky aioli. Buen provecho!

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 200°C (fan)/Gas 6

2) Cut the potatoes (skins on) into bite-sized cubes. Add the potato cubes to a baking tray with a drizzle of vegetable oil and a pinch of salt and pepper. Put the tray in the oven for 25-30 min or until the potatoes are golden and crispy – these are your crispy potatoes

3) Deseed the red and yellow peppers and cut them into thick strips

4) Peel and roughly slice the red onion

5) Add the pepper strips and sliced red onion to a separate baking tray with a drizzle of vegetable oil and a pinch of salt. Put the tray in the oven for 20 min or until the vegetables are soft and tender

6) While the vegetables are in the oven, boil a kettle

7) Dice the tomato

8) Chop the parsley finely, including the stalks

9) Peel and finely chop the garlic

10) Dissolve the tomato paste in 100ml [200ml] boiled water and add the chilli flakes – this is your spicy stock

11) Heat a large, wide-based pan with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the diced tomato with a pinch of salt and sugar and cook for 3-4 min or until the tomatoes have broken down

12) Add the spicy stock to the pan and cook for 3-4 min further or until thickened – this is your bravas sauce

13) While the sauce thickens, cut the chicken breasts lengthways into 4 [8] strips

14) Combine the ground coriander, smoked paprika, 1 tsp [2 tsp] sugar and a generous pinch of salt on a plate

15) Add the chicken strips to the spices and mix until they are fully coated

16) Heat a separate large, wide-based pan with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the chicken strips and cook for 4-5 min on each side or until the chicken is slightly blackened on the outside and cooked through – this is your smoky chicken

17) Meanwhile, combine the mayonnaise, chopped garlic, 1 tsp [2 tsp] cold water and a pinch of salt in a small bowl – this is your aioli

18) Once the peppers and onion are done, add the chopped parsley to the tray and give everything a good mix up – these are your roasted vegetables

19) Serve the smoky chicken with the crispy potatoes and roasted vegetables to the side. Top the crispy potatoes with the bravas sauce and drizzle the aioli all over
