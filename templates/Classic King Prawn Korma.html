Classic King Prawn Korma

Dinner, Indian, Seafood

Total Time: 20 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 brown onion

2 garlic cloves

15g fresh root ginger

10g coriander

1 tbsp curry powder

130g basmati rice

3 cardamom pods

171g raw peeled king prawns

50g solid coconut cream

Description

For this quick, easy twist on a creamy korma, you'll simmer succulent king prawns in a fragrant coconut cream sauce, and spoon it over cardamom rice. Divine!

Directions

1) Boil half a kettle

2) Crush the cardamom pods open by squashing them with the side of a knife

3) Heat a pot with a lid, with a large knob of butter over a medium heat. Once hot, add the crushed cardamom pods and cook for 1 min or until fragrant

4) Add the basmati rice and a pinch of salt to the pot and cook for a further 1 min, then add 300ml [600ml] cold water and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-15 min or until all the water has been absorbed and the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving – this is your cardamom rice

5) While the rice is cooking, peel and finely dice the brown onion, ginger and garlic

6) Heat a large, wide-based pan with a drizzle of vegetable oil and a large knob of butter over a medium heat. Once hot, add the diced onion and a generous pinch of salt and cook for 3-5 min or until soft

7) Once soft, add the chopped ginger, chopped garlic and curry powder and cook for a further 1 min

8) Meanwhile, chop the coriander finely, including the stalks

9) Roughly chop the coconut cream and dissolve in 250ml [500ml] boiled water – this is your coconut stock

10) Add the coconut stock to the pan and cook for 2-3 min or until a thick sauce remains

11) Once the sauce has thickened, add the king prawns and cook for a further 4-5 min or until the prawns are fully cooked. Season generously with both salt and pepper – this is your king prawn korma

12) Serve the king prawn korma over the cardamom rice (discard the cardamom pods!) and garnish with the chopped coriander
