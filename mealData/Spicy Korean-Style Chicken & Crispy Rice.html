Spicy Korean-Style Chicken & Crispy Rice

Chicken, Korean

Total Time: 20 Servings: 2 Source: Gousto

Ingredients

1 red pepper

1 garlic clove

15g fresh root ginger

1 spring onion

300g chicken thighs

150g spring greens

1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes

1 carrot

130g basmati rice

1.5 tbsp Gochujang (or sriracha hot chilli sauce)

1 tbsp toasted sesame oil

1 tbsp soy sauce

1 tbsp rice vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)

3g cornflour

Description

For perfect crispy rice every time, keep an eagle eye on your pot while you make this Korean-inspired “dak galbi”. It's a red-hot stir-fry with juicy chicken thighs, vegetables and that delicious rice.

Directions

1) Deseed the pepper and dice

2) Heat a large, wide-based pan with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the diced pepper and cook for 6-7 min or until softened

3) Meanwhile, add a drizzle of vegetable oil to a separate large, wide-based pan with a matching lid. Add the basmati rice and 300ml cold water and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered for 12-15 min or until all the water has been absorbed

4) Remove the lid and cook over a high heat for 3-4 min further until the rice is starting to crisp on the bottom of the pan – this is your crispy rice

5) While the rice is cooking, top, tail, and grate the carrot and set aside.

6) Rip the leaves off the spring greens, discard the tough stalks, then layer the leaves over each other, roll them up and slice into thin strips. Set aside with the carrots.

7) Cut the chicken thighs into rough bite-sized pieces.

8) Once the pepper has softened, add the minced garlic and ginger to the pan and cook for 1 min. Transfer the softened pepper, ginger and garlic to a food processor.

9) Return the pan to a medium-high heat with a drizzle of vegetable oil. Once hot, add the chicken thigh pieces with a pinch of salt and cook for 2-3 min or until browned all over

10) Add the chilli flakes, gochujang, sesame oil, soy sauce, rice vinegar and 1 tsp sugar to the peppers and blitz until smooth – this is your Korean sauce.

10) Add the grated carrot and sliced spring greens and cook for a further 4-5 min or until tender

11) Add the cornflour to the pan and give it a good mix up. Add the Korean sauce and cook for 5-6 min or until the sauce has thickened and the chicken is cooked through – this is your spicy Korean-style chicken

12) Meanwhile, trim, then slice the spring onion

13) Serve the spicy Korean-style chicken with the crispy rice to the side. Garnish with the sliced spring onion
