Roasted Stuffed Butternut Squash

Dinner, Vegetarian

Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 1 hour 15 minutes Servings: Serving: 1 Source: Minimalistbaker.com

Ingredients

135g bulgar wheat

2 tbsp vegetable oil

210g sliced shiitake mushrooms ( or chestnut?)

1 tbsp soy sauce

2 cloves garlic, minced

135g chopped kale or spinach

60g roughly chopped walnuts (or other nut or seed of choice) (optional)

1 large butternut squash

1 pinch sea salt

240ml balsamic vinegar

Directions

1) Preheat oven to 200 C and line a baking tray

2) Prepare bulgur wheat by adding to medium saucepan with 500ml water and bringing to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce heat to a low simmer, cover, and cook for 18-20 minutes, or until fluffy and the water is absorbed. Remove lid and let cool completely (uncovered) on the stovetop.

3) Halve the squash lengthwise, scoop out seeds then brush with oil. Place cut-side down on a lined baking sheet or baking pan.

4) Bake squash for 15 minutes, then flip the squash over to cut-side up. Bake for another 30-45 minutes or until a knife easily pierces the squash. It's better for the squash to be on the more tender side than firm, in our experience.

5) In the meantime, prepare your balsamic reduction by adding balsamic vinegar to a small saucepan and bringing to a low boil over medium high heat.

6) Once bubbling, reduce heat to a simmer and cook for about 12-15 minutes. You’ll know it’s done when it’s reduced in volume by about half, it appears syrupy, and it has visible bubbles on the top. Set aside to cool. It will thicken as it cools.

7) Once your quinoa is cooked and cooled, heat a large frying pan over medium heat, once hot add oil and quinoa. Sauté for 5-8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly crispy and browned. Season with half the soy sauce for flavor. Then remove from skillet and set aside.

8) To the still hot skillet add the mushrooms and the other half of the soy sauce. Sauté for 2-3 minutes, or until browned and reduced in size.

9) Then add garlic and kale and walnuts (optional) and sauté for another 1-2 minutes or until kale is just wilted.

10) Add quinoa back to the pan and toss to coat. Set aside.

11) Once your squash is roasted, place cut-side up on the baking sheet or dish and fill to the brim with quinoa filling. Then place back in the oven to roast another 5 minutes.
