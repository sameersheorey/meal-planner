Cambodian Chicken & Mango Curry

Cambodian, Chicken

Prep Time: 30 min Total Time: 30 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 shallot

1 lime

15g fresh root ginger

320g chicken thighs

1 tsp nigella seeds

5g basil

130g basmati rice

1/2 chicken stock cube

1/2 tsp ground turmeric

1 green chilli

1 mango

50g solid coconut cream

Description

For this sweet and fiery Cambodian-inspired curry, you'll simmer chicken in fresh homemade curry paste with chilli, basil, ginger and juicy mango. Garnish with lime and chilli, and serve. Delicious!

Directions

1) Boil a kettle

2) Cut the green chilli in half lengthways and deseed. Peel the ginger and roughly chop. Peel and roughly chop the shallot. Slice the lime into 4 wedges.

3) Add the basmati rice and 300ml [600ml] cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has absorbed and the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove the pot from the heat and keep covered until serving.

4) Meanwhile, chop the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces

5) Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a high heat. Once hot, add the chopped chicken with a pinch of salt and cook for 2-3 min or until lightly browned

6) Whilst the chicken is cooking, add the basil, chopped shallot, ginger and 1/2 green chilli to a food processor. Crumble in the coconut cream, add the juice of 2 lime wedges and blitz until smooth – this is your curry paste.

7) Once the chicken is lightly browned, add the curry paste to the pan with the ground turmeric and cook for 2-3 min or until fragrant

8) Meanwhile, dissolve the stock cube in 150ml [300ml] boiled water

9) Once fragrant, add the chicken stock to the pan and cook for 4-5 min until the chicken is cooked through (no pink meat!)

10) Meanwhile, top, tail and peel the mango. Stand the mangoupright and very carefully slice the flesh away from the stone. Chop the mango flesh into small bite-sized pieces, discarding the stone.

11) Slice the remaining green chilli finely

12) Stir the chopped mango through the chicken curry and serve over the cooked basmati rice. Garnish with the nigella seeds, chopped chilli and remaining lime wedges
