Mary's Spiced Whole Orange Cake

Cakes

Source: GBBO Everyday

Ingredients

For the cake:

1 small, thin-skinned orange

275g self-raising flour

2 tsp baking powder

275g caster sugar

275g baking spread, from the fridge

4 medium free-range eggs, at room temperature

1 tsp ground cinnamon

1 tsp mixed spice

For the orange icing:

50g unsalted butter, softened

175g icing sugar, sieved

250g full-fat mascarpone cheese

2 tbsp orange pulp, reserved from above

To decorate:

1 large, thin-skinned orange

50g caster sugar

Description

This cake has a wonderful citrus flavour. Add a little more spice if you want it to be very spicy. You'll need a food processor to make it.

Directions

1) For the decoration, using a canelle knife, peel thick long strips of orange peel from the large orange. If you don't have a canelle knife, carefully slice away the rind and then cut into pencil-thick strips.

2) Place in a saucepan, cover with boiling water, add half of the sugar and boil for one minute

3) Drain the strips and place on non-stick paper. Scatter over the remaining sugar and toss together, leave in a warm place to dry out for as long as possible or overnight until crisp.

4) Grease and line the bases of two 20cm sandwich tins with non-stick baking paper. Preheat the oven to 160C fan/Gas 4

5) For the cake, place the whole orange in a small saucepan, cover with boiling water and boil for 30 minutes, or until soft. Leave to cool. When the orange is cold, cut in half and remove any pips.

6) Roughly chop the orange and blend, including the skin, in a food processor until medium-chunky in texture. Transfer to a bowl and set aside, reserving two tablespoons for the icing.

7) Add the remaining cake ingredients to the food processor (no need to rinse out) and blend until just smooth (be careful not to over-beat the mixture).

8) Remove the blade and carefully stir in the pulp that you have set aside.

9) Divide between the two prepared tins and level the tops. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until well risen, lightly golden-brown and shrinking away from the sides of the tins.

10) Leave to cool for a few minutes and then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Peel off the baking paper.

11) For the icing, place the butter in a mixing bowl and, using an electric hand whisk, gradually beat in the icing sugar until smooth. Add the mascarpone and whisk together until smooth and creamy. Add the reserved 2 tbsp orange pulp and mix until smooth.

12) Slice each cake in half to make four layers. Divide the icing into four and then stack the layers of cake - icing between each layer and finishing with icing on the top.

13) Decorate the cake with the candied orange rind and serve. This cake is best eaten on the day it is made but can be kept for up to 3 days in a cool place.
