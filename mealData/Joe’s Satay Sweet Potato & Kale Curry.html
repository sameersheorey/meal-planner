Joe’s Satay Sweet Potato & Kale Curry

★★

Vegetarian

Total Time: 25 Servings: 2 Source: Gousto

Ingredients

1 brown onion

100g red lentils

1 lime

15g ginger

25g roasted peanuts

1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes

1 tbsp curry powder

5g coriander

80g shredded kale

1/2 vegetable stock cube

1 medium sweet potato

104g smooth peanut butter

16g tomato paste

80g natural yoghurt

Description

This hearty curry's from my Veggie Lean in 15 book! You'll add slow release energy with sweet potatoes and thicken it with lentils for a Gousto twist. Finish with a squeeze of lime. Winner!

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 200°C (fan)/Gas 7

2) Chop the sweet potato (skin on) into small bite-sized pieces and add to a baking tray with 1 tbsp vegetable oil and a pinch of salt and give it a good mix up. Put the tray in the oven for 15-20 min or until the sweet potato is cooked and golden

3) While the sweet potato is cooking, boil a kettle.

4) Peel and finely chop the brown onion. Heat a large wide-based pan (with a lid) with 1 tbsp vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the chopped onion with a pinch of salt and cook for 4-5 min or until softened

5) Peel and finely chop the ginger

6) Dissolve vegetable stock cube in 600ml [1L] boiled water

7) Once the onion has softened, add the curry powder, chilli flakes, tomato paste and chopped ginger and cook for 1 min or until fragrant

8) Rinse the red lentils, add them to the pan with the vegetable stock and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat and cook, covered, for 10-15 min or until the lentils are cooked and tender

9) Meanwhile, chop the coriander roughly including the stalks, keeping the stalks separate from the leaves. Chop the lime in half

10) Once the lentils are cooked, add the shredded kale to the pan and cook, covered, for a further 3-5 min or until the kale has wilted

11) Once the kale has wilted, add the peanut butter with the coriander stalks (save the leaves for garnish) and the juice of 1/2 lime and stir it all together – this is your satay kale curry

12) Cut the remaining lime into wedges

13) Serve the roasted sweet potato over the satay kale curry, finish with a dollop of natural yoghurt and garnish with the coriander leaves, roasted peanuts and a lime wedge
