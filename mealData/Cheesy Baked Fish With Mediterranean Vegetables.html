Cheesy Baked Fish With Mediterranean Vegetables

★★★

Fish

Total Time: 35 Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

2 red onions

10g basil

1 courgette

40g cheddar cheese

125g cherry tomatoes

2 x 100g basa fillets

1/2 vegetable stock cube

16g tomato paste

30g panko breadcrumbs

3 white potatoes

Description

This dishy fish supper is simply delicious, and inspired by the Mediterranean sun. While the fish and potatoes brown in the oven, you'll braise colourful vegetables to serve alongside. What a catch!

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 200°C (fan)/ Gas 7

2) Boil a kettle

3) Cut the potatoes (skins on) into bite-sized pieces. Add to a tray with a drizzle of olive oil and season with salt and pepper and give everything a good mix up. Put the tray in the oven for 20-25 min or until the potatoes are crisp

4) Peel and chop the red onions roughly. Top, tail and chop the courgette roughly into bite-sized pieces. Chop the cherry tomatoes in half.

5) Dissolve 1/2 vegetable stock cube in 50ml boiled water

6) Grate the cheddar cheese into a bowl. Add the panko breadcrumbs and season generously with salt and pepper. Add a drizzle of vegetable oil and give everything a good mix up – this is your cheesy breadcrumb mixture

7) Pat the basa fillets dry with kitchen paper and add them to a baking tray (use tin foil to avoid mess!). Sprinkle the cheesy breadcrumb mixture over the fillets until they're totally covered and put the tray in the oven for 12-15 min or until the breadcrumb mixture is starting to turn golden and the fish is cooked – this is your cheesy baked fish

Tip: Your fish is cooked when it turns opaque and flakes easily

8) While the fish is cooking, heat a large, wide-based pan with a drizzle of olive oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the chopped onion, courgette and tomato paste and cook for 2-3 min or until starting to soften

9) Add the cherry tomatoes and cook for 4-5 min further or until all the vegetables have softened

10) Add the vegetable stock and cook for 1-2 min further. Season generously with salt and pepper – these are your Mediterranean vegetables

11) Once done, tear the basil leaves into your Mediterranean vegetables. Serve the cheesy baked fish over the Mediterranean vegetables with the roast potatoes to the side
