Tandoori Fish, Mint Raita & Kachumber Salad

Dinner, Fish, Indian

Total Time: 20 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 shallot

2 tomatoes

1/2 cucumber

1 tsp nigella seeds

1 tbsp mint sauce

1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes

1 tbsp curry powder

200g basa fillets

130g basmati rice

80g natural yoghurt

Description

For this quick tandoori-style dish, you'll char the fish under the grill to give it smoky flavour. Serve alongside a cooling mint raita, and a tomato and cucumber salad that's known as a 'kachumber'.

Directions

1) Preheat your grill to a high heat

2) Add the basmati rice, nigella seeds, a pinch of salt and 300ml [600ml] cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has absorbed and the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving

3) Cut the unsmoked basa fillets in half lengthways, and then chop into bite-sized pieces

4) Combine half of the natural yoghurt with the curry powder, chilli flakes, a generous pinch of salt and 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] olive oil in a bowl

5) Add the chopped basa pieces and mix well until they're completely coated – this is your tandoori fish

6) Cover a baking tray with tin foil and drizzle with olive oil (to prevent the fish from sticking)

7) Add the tandoori fish to the baking tray and put it under the grill for 6-7 min or until the fish is cooked through and starting to char

8) Meanwhile, cut the cucumber in half lengthways, scrape the seeds out and set aside for later. Dice the cucumber

9) Peel and chop the shallot

10) Dice the tomatoes

11) In a bowl combine the diced cucumber, chopped shallot and diced tomato with 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] olive oil. Season with a pinch of salt – this is your kachumber salad

12) Combine the cucumber seeds, remaining yoghurt and mint sauce in a mixing bowl. Season with a large pinch of salt – this is your mint raita

13) Serve the tandoori fish with the cooked basmati rice, kachumber salad and mint raita to the side
