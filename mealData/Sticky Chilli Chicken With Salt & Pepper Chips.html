Sticky Chilli Chicken With Salt & Pepper Chips

★★★★

Chicken, Chinese, Dinner

Total Time: 35 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 red chilli

1 red pepper

2 garlic cloves

15g fresh root ginger

1 spring onion

25g chilli jam

5g coriander

4 tbsp cornflour

1 tbsp honey

2 tbsp soy sauce sachet

1 tbsp Chinese rice wine

4 white potatoes

250g diced chicken thigh

Description

Wake up your tastebuds with this sticky chilli sensation. You'll smother crispy chicken in a sweet and spicy sauce, then add a side of crispy salt and pepper chips to serve. Winner!

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 180°C (fan)/Gas 6

2) Cut the potatoes (skins on) into chips. Add the chips to a baking tray with a generous drizzle of vegetable oil, lots of pepper and a sprinkle of salt. Give everything a good mix up and put the tray in the oven for 20-25 min or until golden and crisp

3) While the chips are cooking, heat a large, wide-based pan with a very large drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium-high heat.

4) Add the diced chicken thighs to a bowl with the cornflour and give it a good mix up until all the chicken is coated

5) Once the pan is very hot, add the coated chicken along with half of the soy sauce (save the rest for later!) and cook for 5-6 min on each side until golden and crispy and the chicken is cooked through – this is your crispy chicken

6) Once crispy, transfer to a plate and set aside and reserve the pan

7) Cut the red pepper[s] in half and chop into small cubes

8) Peel and finely chop the garlic and ginger

9) Finely slice the red chilli into rounds

10) Trim, then finely slice the spring onion

11) Return the reserved pan to a medium-high heat with any remaining oil. Add the chopped red pepper, ginger and half of the chopped garlic (save the rest for later!) and cook for 3-4 min or until fragrant

12) Meanwhile, add the remaining soy sauce to a bowl with the honey, chilli jam and Chinese rice wine – this is your sticky chilli sauce

13) Add the sticky chilli sauce to the pan and cook for 3 min or until the sauce begins to thicken and the pepper has softened

14) While the sauce thickens, chop the coriander finely, including the stalks

15) Once thickened, add the crispy chicken to the pan and cook for 1 min further until the chicken is coated in the sauce – this is your sticky chilli chicken

16) Once the chips are cooked, add the remaining chopped garlic, chopped spring onion and chilli rounds to the tray and give everything a good mix up – these are your salt & pepper chips

17) Serve the sticky chilli chicken with the salt & pepper chips to the side. Garnish the chicken with the chopped coriander
