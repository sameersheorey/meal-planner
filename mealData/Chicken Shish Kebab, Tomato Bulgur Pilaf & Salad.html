Chicken Shish Kebab, Tomato Bulgur Pilaf & Salad

★★★

Chicken, Turkish

Total Time: 50 Servings: 2 Source: Gousto

Ingredients

1 lemon

1 red onion

1 tomato

300g chicken thighs

4 skewers

50g salad leaves

2 tsp ground cumin

2 tsp ground paprika

1 carrot

1 chicken stock cube

60g bulgur wheat

16g tomato paste

80g natural yoghurt

Description

These Turkish-inspired kebabs are hold-me-back good! You'll marinate succulent chicken in spices, grill and serve with lemon yoghurt, tomato bulgur pilaf and a vibrant salad. Lezzetli (delicious)!

Directions

1) Slice the chicken thighs into thin strips, then add them to a bowl with the ground paprika, ground cumin, the juice of 1/2 lemon and a drizzle of olive oil. Season with a generous pinch of salt and pepper and give everything a good mix up

2) Put the chicken strips in the fridge to marinate until later

3) Meanwhile, dice the red onion as finely as you can. Heat a large, wide-based pan with a lid with a drizzle of olive oil over a low heat. Once hot, add the diced onion and cook for 6 min or until softened

4) Boil a kettle

5) Soak your skewers in cold water (this prevents them from burning whilst cooking)

6) Dissolve the chicken stock cube and tomato paste in 400ml [750ml] boiled water – this is your tomato stock

7) Once the onion has softened, add the bulgur wheat and sultanas and cook for 1 min or until coated in the oil

8) Add the tomato stock to the pan and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cook, covered, for 15 min or until the water has absorbed and the bulgur is cooked – this is your tomato bulgur pilaf

9) Meanwhile, preheat the grill to high

10) Thread the marinated chicken strips evenly onto 4 skewers, leaving a small gap between each piece – these are your chicken shish kebabs

11) Add the chicken shish kebabs to a baking tray (use tin foil to avoid mess!). Put the tray under the grill for 12-14 min, turning once halfway, or until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.

12) Meanwhile, add the natural yoghurt to a bowl with a pinch of salt and a small squeeze of lemon juice (save the rest for later!) – this is your lemon yoghurt

13) Meanwhile, grate the carrot and dice the tomato

14) Add the remaining lemon juice to a large bowl with 2 tbsp olive oil and a pinch of salt. Add the grated carrot, diced tomato and baby leaf salad and give everything a good mix up

15) Drizzle the lemon yoghurt over the chicken shish kebabs and serve with the tomato bulgur pilaf and the salad to the side
