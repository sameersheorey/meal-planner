Christmas Cake

Christmas

Source: Granny

Ingredients

10 oz currants

10 oz sultanas

7 oz raisins

5 oz glace cherries chopped

4 oz chopped mixed peel

4 oz flaked almonds

10 oz plain flour

1 level tsp grated nutmeg

1 level tsp mixed spice

1 level tsp ground cinnamon

10 oz Bertoli (or margarine of your choice)

10 oz Dark soft muscavado sugar

Finely grated rind of 1 lemon

6 eggs

Directions

1) Line 9 inch (23 cm) tin with double layer of greaseproof paper

2) After picking the fruit over for stalks etc. I put it in a bowl with a couple of tablespoons of brandy, cover it with cling film and leave it overnight or longer if it is not convenient to continue!

3) Mix all the fruit and nuts together

4) Sieve flour, nutmeg, mixed spice and cinnamon together into a basin

5) Beat the margarine in a mixing bowl until soft

6) Add the sugar to the margarine and cream thoroughly

7) Add the lemon and beat in

8) Add the eggs one at a time and beat in thoroughly

9) Fold in the mixed fruit and the flour mixture, half at a time, gently and thoroughly until well mixed.

10) Turn into the prepared tin and smooth top, then make a hollow in the centre.
11) Cook for 3 hours at 120C

12) Remove the cake from the oven when baked. Leave in the tin for 10 minutes. Turn out, remove the paper and cool on a wire tray.

13) Leave for at least a day, then marzipan.

14) Leave for at least another day and ice.

Notes

As I said earlier I protect the cake from burning by tying folded newspaper around the outside of the tin and standing it on a Radio Times on a baking tray. If the top browns quickly place greaseproof paper over the top of the cake halfway through the baking time.
