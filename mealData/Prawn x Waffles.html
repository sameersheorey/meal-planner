Prawn x Waffles

Dinner, Seafood

Servings: 6 Source: Second Helpings - Liam Charles

Ingredients

Curried prawns

375 g pumpkin or butternut squash

375 g potatoes

4 tbsp vegetable oil

1 large onion, diced

3 garlic cloves, minced

10 cm ginger

2 large green peppers, diced

4 tsp mild curry powder

1 tsp turmeric

1 tsp garam masala

1 tsp coriander

2 tsp all-purpose seasoning

1 Scotch bonnet chilli

600g peeled raw prawns


Waffles

3 large eggs

450 ml milk

340 g self-raising flour

3 tsp baking powder

1 tsp smoked paprika

1 tsp black pepper

1 tsp salt

225 g unsalted butter

25g chives


Mango chutney to serve


Directions

1) Peel the pumpkin and potatoes and cut into 2cm chunks. Steam for 20 minutes until almost tender.

2) Heat the vegetable oil in a large frying pan over a medium heat, add the onion, garlic, ginger and green peppers and fry gently for 6-7 minutes until the onions and peppers are soft and tender.

3) Reserve a large cupful of water from the steamer, drain the pan and add the pumpkin, potato, onion and pepper mix, spices and half the water.

4) Place over a medium-low heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes until pumpkin and potato are completely tender and the sauce is thick (add the rest of the water if needed). Season to taste.

5) Melt the waffle butter and leave to cool

6) Cut a few slits in the chilli

7) Turn up the heat, drop the chilli and prawns into the pan and stir well. Once the curry starts to boil, reduce the heat again and cook the prawns for 10-15 minutes until they turn pink and are cooked through.

8) Crack the eggs into a large bowl, add the milk and whisk.

9) Add the flour, baking powder, smoked paprika, salt and pepper and whisk again until well incorporated.

10) Add the melted butter, chives and stir

11) Preheat the George Foreman grill with the front raised on a book,

12) Add waffle batter and cook for about 5 minutes, turn and cook for another 5 minutes. Repeat until all batter is used.

13) Serve with mango chutney
