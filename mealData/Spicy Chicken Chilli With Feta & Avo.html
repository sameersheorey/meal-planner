Spicy Chicken Chilli With Feta & Avo

Chicken, Mexican

Total Time: 25 Servings: 2 Source: Gousto

Ingredients

1 lime

1 garlic clove

1 spring onion

1 tsp smoked paprika

1 tsp ground cumin

1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes

100g brown rice

1 avocado

30g Greek feta cheese

1 tbsp tomato paste

1 can of black beans (185g)

2 x 125g British chicken breast fillets

20g chipotle paste

Directions

1) Boil half a kettle

2) Rinse the brown rice, add it to a pot with plenty of cold water and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium and cook for 15-20 min or until it's tender with a slight bite. Once cooked, drain, return it to the pot and keep covered until serving.

3) Add the smoked paprika and ground cumin to a plate with a pinch of salt and 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil. Mix it all up, then add the chicken breasts and turn until well coated

4) Heat a large, wide-based pan (with a lid) over a high heat with 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil. Once hot, add the coated chicken breasts and cook for 3 min on each side or until browned

5) Whilst the chicken is browning, peel and finely chop the garlic

6) Dissolve the tomato paste in 200ml [350ml] boiled water and add the chipotle paste - this is your chipotle stock

7) Once the chicken has browned, add the garlic and cook for 30 secs. Add the chipotle stock, drained black beans and chilli flakes and bring to the boil

8) Cook, covered, for a further 12-15 min or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened

9) Cut the avocado in half lengthways, around the stone, remove the stone using a teaspoon, scoop the avocado out of the skin using a spoon and dice roughly

10) Trim, then slice the spring onion. Crumble the feta into large pieces. Cut the lime in half

11) Combine the diced avocado, crumbled feta and sliced spring onion in a small bowl, Add the juice of 1/2 lime and stir it all together – this is your feta & avocado salsa

12) Cut the remaining lime into wedges

13) Once the chicken is cooked, transfer it to a clean board and shred apart using two forks. Return the shredded chicken to the sauce and give everything a good mix up - this is your chicken chilli

14) Serve the chicken chilli with the brown rice and feta & avocado salsa to the side, and a wedge of lime.
