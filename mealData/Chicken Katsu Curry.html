Chicken Katsu Curry

Chicken, Japanese

Prep Time: 40 min Total Time: 40 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 shallot

130g brown rice

1 free-range egg

15g fresh root ginger

1 tsp soy sauce

50g baby leaf salad

1 tbsp mango chutney

1 tbsp curry powder

1 carrot

1 tbsp rice vinegar

30g panko breadcrumbs

250g chicken breast fillets

Description

Take a walk on the mild side with a crispy chicken katsu curry. You'll serve this Japanese-style supper with an umami-rich curry sauce, wholesome brown rice and colourful salad to the side. Katsu-perb!

Directions

1) Rinse the brown rice, add it to a pot with plenty of cold water and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium and cook for 15-20 min or until it's tender with a slight bite. Once cooked, drain, return it to the pot and keep covered until serving

2) Top, tail and peel half the carrot into a pile of ribbons. Grate the remaining carrot

3) Peel and finely chop the ginger and the shallot

4) Crack the egg into a shallow bowl and beat lightly with a fork

5) Add 2 tbsp [4 tbsp] flour to a plate and season with salt and pepper, then add the panko breadcrumbs to another plate

6) Coat the chicken breasts in the flour, tap off the excess, then add it into the beaten egg and finally press it into the breadcrumbs firmly to evenly coat all over

7) Boil a kettle

8) Heat a large, wide-based pan with a matching lid, with 2-3 tbsp vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the grated carrot, ginger and shallot and cook for 3 min

9) Add the curry powder and cook for a further 2 min

10) Add 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] flour and cook for 1 min, stirring to coat the vegetables evenly

11) Add 300ml [600ml] boiled water and reduce the heat to low

12) Add the soy sauce and stir through the mango chutney

13) Cook for 5-7 min or until the sauce has thickened to a curry-like consistency. Season with a generous pinch of salt, cover with a lid and set aside for later – this is your katsu sauce

14) Heat a separate, large, wide-based pan with a very generous drizzle of vegetable oil (enough to cover the base of the pan) over a medium-high heat. Once hot, add the coated chicken and cook for 6-7 min on each side pressing down firmly with a spatula until the outside is golden and the chicken is cooked through

15) In a large bowl combine the rice vinegar with 2 tbsp [4 tbsp] olive oil and a pinch of salt – this is your dressing

16) Wash the baby leaf salad, then add the salad and the carrot ribbons to the bowl with the dressing and give it a good mix up

17) Slice the cooked chicken into strips

18) Spoon the katsu sauce onto the plate and serve the sliced chicken over the sauce with the cooked brown rice and salad to the side
