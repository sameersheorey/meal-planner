Sesame-Crusted Fish With Chilli-Lime Noodles

Dinner, Fish, Japanese

Prep Time: 20 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 lime

15g fresh root ginger

2 spring onions

25g chilli jam

1 tsp dried chilli flakes

2 wholewheat noodle nests

1 tbsp white miso paste

80g tenderstem broccoli

2 basa fillets

1 tbsp soy sauce

5g black sesame seeds

5g toasted sesame seeds

Description

In this speedy dish, you'll top basa fillets with miso paste and black & white sesame seeds to create sesame-crusted fish, oven-baked for ease. Served over sweet chilli wholewheat noodles packed with freshness from tenderstem broccoli, spring onion & a squeeze of lime.

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 200°C (fan)/ Gas 7

2) Boil a kettle

3) Pat the basa fillets dry with kitchen paper and add them to a baking tray. Spread the miso paste evenly over the basa fillets with the back of a teaspoon

4) Sprinkle the black and white sesame seeds over the basa fillets and press down firmly with your hand so that they stick. Drizzle the fish with vegetable oil and put the tray in the oven for 8-10 min or until cooked through 

5) Meanwhile, add the wholewheat noodles to a pot and cover with boiled water until fully submerged. Bring to the boil over a high heat and cook for 5 min

6) Meanwhile, peel and finely chop the ginger and add to a bowl with the soy sauce, chilli jam, dried chilli flakes, 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] vegetable oil and the juice of 1/2 [1] lime . Give everything a good old mix up – this is your sweet chilli dressing 

7) Once the noodles have been cooking for 5 min, add the tenderstem broccoli and cook for 2 min further, or until everything's tender with a slight bite. Once cooked, drain everything and return it to the pot 

8) Trim, then slice the spring onions 

9) Add the sweet chilli dressing to the noodles with the chopped spring onion and give everything a good old mix up – these are your sweet chilli noodles

10) Cut the remaining lime into wedges 

11) Serve the sesame-crusted fish over the sweet chilli noodles. Garnish with a wedge of lime
