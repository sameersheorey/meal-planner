Creamy Haddock Linguine With Tomatoes & Chilli

Dinner, Fish, Italian

Prep Time: 20 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 garlic clove

125g cherry tomatoes

40g Cornish clotted cream

1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes

5g chives

200g haddock

1/2 vegetable stock cube

190g linguine

1 tbsp Chinese rice wine

1 tbsp tomato paste

Description

To rustle up this punchy pasta, you'll pan-fry haddock bites, and add them to a rich tomato sauce with garlic and chilli. Toss with linguine and serve with more chives. Delicious!

Directions

1) Cut the haddock into bite-size pieces, pat dry with kitchen paper and season with salt and pepper

2) Boil a kettle, then heat a large, wide-based pan with 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] olive oil over a high heat. Once hot, add the haddock to the pan and cook for 3-4 min, turning halfway, until cooked through

3) Transfer the cooked haddock bites to a plate and save the pan for later

4) Add the linguine to a pot of boiled water with a pinch of salt, bring to the boil over a high heat and cook for 8-10 min or until cooked with a slight bite. Once cooked, drain the linguine and set aside until later

5) While the linguine is cooking, reboil half a kettle

6) Peel and finely chop the garlic

7) Chop the cherry tomatoes in half

8) Return the pan to a medium heat (if it's looking a little dry, add a drizzle of olive oil). Once hot, add the chopped garlic and chilli flakes and cook for 1 min

9) Add the halved cherry tomatoes with a very generous pinch of salt and cook for 2 min further

10) Dissolve the tomato paste and 1/2 [1] vegetable stock cube in 200ml [400ml] boiled water – this is your tomato stock

11) Chop the chives finely

12) Add the Chinese rice wine to the pan and cook for 30 secs, then pour in the tomato stock

13) Cook for 5-7 min or until reduced to a pasta sauce-like consistency. Season with a pinch of pepper and a generous pinch of salt and remove from the heat

14) Stir through the clotted cream and half of the chopped chives

15) Add the drained linguine and haddock bites to the sauce and give everything a good mix up – this is your creamy haddock linguine

Tip: If the sauce is too thin, return it to the heat and stir until the sauce sticks to the pasta

16) Serve the creamy haddock linguine in bowls and sprinkle over the remaining chopped chives
