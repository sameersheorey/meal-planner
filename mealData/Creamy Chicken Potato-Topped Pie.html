Creamy Chicken Potato-Topped Pie

British, Chicken, Dinner

Total Time: 45 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 leek

2 garlic cloves

150g spring greens

1 carrot

50g soft cheese

5.5g Dijon mustard

1 chicken stock cube

4 white potatoes

250g chicken breast fillets

Description

Hearty and oh-so-creamy, this deliciously crispy mash-topped pie is packed with chicken breast, sweet leeks and carrot. Serve with garlicky greens.

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 200°C (fan)/Gas 7

2) Boil a kettle and chop the potatoes (skins on) into large bite-sized pieces. Add the chopped potatoes to a pot with plenty of boiled water and a pinch of salt and bring to the boil over a high heat

3) Cook for 12-15 min or until fork-tender, then drain and return to the pot to steam dry

4) Whilst the potatoes are boiling, add the chicken breasts to a baking tray with drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper. Put the tray in the oven for 15 min or until cooked through

5) Re-boil a kettle

6) Chop the leek in half lengthways, then wash to remove any grit from between the leaves, then top, tail and slice finely

7) Top, tail and dice the carrot

8) Heat a large, wide-based pan with 15g [30g] butter over a medium heat. Once melted, add the chopped leek and carrot with a pinch of salt and cook for 4-5 min or until beginning to soften

9) Meanwhile, peel and finely chop (or grate) the garlic

10) Dissolve the chicken stock cube and Dijon mustard in 250ml [400ml] boiled water

11) Once the vegetables have softened, add half the chopped garlic with 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] flour and cook for 30 secs

12) Add the stock and soft cheese, bring to the boil over a high heat and cook for 3-4 min or until thickened

13) Once the chicken is cooked, transfer it to a clean board and shred it apart using two forks

14) Return the drained potatoes to a low heat with a knob of butter and a small splash of milk. Season with a generous pinch of salt and pepper and mash until smooth

15) Add the pulled chicken to the sauce with a generous grind of black pepper and give everything a good mix up – this is your creamy chicken pie filling

16) Transfer the creamy chicken pie filling to an oven-proof dish and top with the mash. Put the dish in the oven for 15-20 min or until bubbling and golden – this is your creamy chicken potato-topped pie

17) Once the pie is almost done, rip the leaves off the spring greens, discard the tough stalks, then layer the leaves over each other, roll them up and slice very finely

18) Heat a separate large, wide-based pan with a knob of butter over a medium heat

19) Once hot, add the sliced spring greens and remaining chopped garlic and cook for 3-4 min or until tender with a bite – these are your garlicky greens

20) Serve the creamy chicken potato-topped pie with the garlicky greens to the side
