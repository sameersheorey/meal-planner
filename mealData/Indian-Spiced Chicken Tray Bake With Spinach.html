Indian-Spiced Chicken Tray Bake With Spinach

Chicken, Dinner, Indian

Total Time: 45 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 lemon

2 red onions

1 red chilli

3 garlic cloves

15g fresh root ginger

320g chicken thighs

1 tsp nigella seeds

1 tsp ground turmeric

1 tbsp curry powder

120g baby leaf spinach

3.8g yellow mustard seeds

4 white potatoes

160g natural yoghurt

Description

For this easy, one-pan traybake, you'll marinate chicken thighs in ginger, yoghurt and curry powder, then roast with turmeric lemon potatoes. What a feast!

Directions

1) Preheat the oven to 200°C (fan)/Gas 7

2) Peel (scrape the skin off with a teaspoon) and finely chop the ginger and add to a large bowl with the curry powder, half the natural yoghurt and a drizzle of vegetable oil. Add the chicken thighs and give everything a good mix up until the chicken is fully coated in the marinade

3) Cut the potatoes (skins on) into small bite-sized pieces

4) Peel and chop the red onion[s] into wedges

5) Crush the garlic with the side of a knife, keeping the skins on

6) Add the chopped potatoes and red onion wedges to a large roasting tray with a generous drizzle of vegetable oil. Sprinkle over the ground turmeric and yellow mustard seeds. Season with a generous pinch of salt and pepper and stir it all together

7) Cut the lemon[s] into wedges. Squeeze half the lemon wedges over the potatoes and add the squeezed wedges to the tray (save the remaining wedges for garnish)

8) Add the crushed garlic to the tray, along with the chicken and any remaining marinade

9) Put the tray in the oven for an initial 30 min

10) Once everything's been cooking for 30 min, remove the tray from the oven, push the chicken to one side of the tray and add the baby leaf spinach

11) Return the tray to the oven for a further 5 min or until the spinach has wilted and the chicken is cooked through – this is your Indian-spiced chicken

12) Meanwhile, slice the red chilli finely

13) Once everything's cooked, remove the Indian-spiced chicken from the tray and stir the wilted spinach through the potatoes and onion wedges

14) Serve the Indian-spiced chicken with the potato, spinach & onion wedges to the side. Dollop with the remaining natural yoghurt. Garnish with the sliced chilli, nigella seeds and lemon wedges
