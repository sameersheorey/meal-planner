Chocolate & Pecan Millionaire Squares

Baking, Biscuits

Prep Time: 30 mins + 4 hrs chilling Cook Time: 25-30 mins Total Time: 5 hrs Servings: 16 Source: BakedIn

Ingredients

180g plain flour

55g soft light brown sugar

20g ground almonds

110g demerara sugar

30g roasted pecans

90g dark chocolate

220g unsalted butter

110ml double cream

Directions

1) Preheat oven to 160 C/ gas mark 4

2) Grease and line a 20x20 square tin

3) To make the shortbread, briefly beat 135g butter until soft, then gradually add the flour, light brown sugar and a pinch of salt

4) Mix in the ground almonds until a soft dough forms

5) Put the dough into the tin and distribute to the corners with your fingers. Press and smooth the dough with the back of a spoon until it is evenly spread

6) Bake the shortbread for 25-30 minutes or until lightly golden.

7) Leave to cool completely in the tin - whilst still slightly warm, if shortbread has receded from edge of tin, use back of spoon to gently push back

8) When the shortbread has cooled roughly chop the pecans.

9) To make the caramel put 50g butter, the demerara sugar and a pinch of salt in a saucepan over a low heat.

10) Once the butter has melted slightly ass 110ml of double cream and stir continuously.

11) Once the sugar has dissolved, turn the heat up slightly and let the sauce bubble for 2-3 minutes before removing from the heat, continue to stir for a few seconds.

12) Add the chopped pecans to the caramel and mix well

13) Pour the caramel over the shortbread and spread to the corners. Chill in fridge for at least 1 hour until fully set.

14) Stir 35g butter and the dark chocolate in a saucepan over a medium heat until melted.

15) Pour the chocolate topping over the cooled caramel and spread to the edges to create a thin layer of chocolate. Use a fork to make a wavy pattern on the surface

16) Chill in the fridge for 2-3 hours

17) Remove from the fridge and rest for 5 minutes before cutting. Use a knife to loosen the bake from the sides of the tin and cut into 1 squares
