Punjabi-Style Black Dal Makhani with Cumin Rice

Indian, Vegetarian

Prep Time: 35 min Total Time: 35 min Servings: 2 Source: Gousto.co.uk

Ingredients

1 red onion

1 red chilli

3 garlic cloves

15g fresh root ginger

2 tsp cumin seeds

40g clotted cream

1 tsp ground turmeric

1 tsp garam masala

5g coriander

100g basmati rice

1/2 vegetable stock cube

250g black beluga lentils (ready-to-eat)

2 tbsp tomato paste

1 can of red kidney beans (400g)

Description

To make our twist on an Indian dal makhani, you'll top cumin rice with black lentils and kidney beans simmered in clotted cream and rich spices. Su'ādī (delicious)!

Directions

1) Peel and finely dice the red onion

2) Heat a wide-based pan with a generous drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium-high heat. Once hot, add half of the cumin seeds and cook for 1-2 min or until starting to become fragrant

3) Add the chopped red onion and ground turmeric and cook for 5-6 min or until starting to soften

4) Meanwhile, peel and finely chop the ginger and garlic. Slice some rings from the red chilli (reserve these for garnish), and chop the rest finely. Chop the coriander, including the stalks, roughly

5) Add the basmati rice, a pinch of salt, the remaining cumin seeds and 250ml [500ml] cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has absorbed and the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving – this is your cumin rice

6) Boil a kettle

7) Once the onions have softened, add the chopped garlic, ginger and chilli and cook for 3-4 min further or until fragrant

8) Meanwhile, drain and rinse the kidney beans

9) Dissolve vegetable stock cube in 400ml [800ml] boiled water

10) Add the tomato paste to the pan and cook for 1-2 min.

11) Add the kidney beans and the black beluga lentils and cook for 2-3 min or until starting to soften, then mash them roughly

Tip: Add a splash of water if it is looking a little dry – this will help to thicken the sauce!

12) Add the vegetable stock and garam masala to the pan and bring to the boil. Cook for 6-8 min further or until the kidney beans are cooked and the sauce has thickened. Season generously with salt

13) Once thickened, add the clotted cream and cook for 1 min further, or until melted – this is your Punjabi-style black dal makhani

14) Serve the Punjabi-style black dal makhani over the cumin rice. Garnish with the reserved chilli rings and chopped coriander
