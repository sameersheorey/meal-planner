Chinese Cashew Chicken & Basmati Rice

Chicken, Chinese

Total Time: 25 Servings: 2 Source: Gousto

Ingredients

2 garlic cloves

15g fresh root ginger

3 spring onions

100g cashew nuts

4 tbsp cornflour

1 carrot

130g basmati rice

15ml toasted sesame oil

20g hoisin sauce

30ml soy sauce

15ml Chinese rice wine

250g diced chicken thigh

Description

Cook up the classic crispy-yet-sticky cashew chicken you know and love – fresh from the pan, with plenty of veggies, and served with fluffy basmati rice. Yum!

Directions

1) Trim, then roughly chop the spring onions

2) Top, tail, peel and slice the carrot in half lengthways then slice diagonally into half-moons

3) Peel and finely chop the ginger and garlic

4) Add the diced chicken thigh to a bowl with the cornflour and and give everything a good mix up until the chicken is fully coated

5) Heat a large, wide-based pan with a very generous drizzle of vegetable oil over a high heat. Once the pan is hot, add the coated chicken thigh pieces and the soy sauce to the pan and cook for 4-5 min on each side until the chicken has started to brown and crisp

6) Meanwhile, add the basmati rice and 300ml cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has absorbed and the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving

7) Once the chicken is lightly browned, add the carrot half-moons and half of the spring onion (save the rest for later!) and cook for 2-3 min until softened

8) Once the carrot and spring onion have softened, reduce the heat to medium, add the chopped ginger, garlic and cashew nuts and cook for 1-2 min or until fragrant

9) Add the Chinese rice wine to the pan and cook for 30 secs

10) Add the hoisin sauce and 150ml [250ml] water to the pan, give everything a good mix up and cook for 2-3 min until thickened and the chicken is cooked through.
Tip: Add another splash of water if it's looking a little thick!

11) Once the sauce has thickened, stir through the toasted sesame oil and remaining spring onion – this is your Chinese cashew chicken

12) Serve the Chinese cashew chicken over the cooked basmati rice
