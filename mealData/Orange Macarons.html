Orange Macarons

Baking

Servings: 35 macarons Source: Bbcgoodfood.com

Ingredients

170g icing sugar

160g ground almonds

120ml egg whites (~4 medium eggs) in 2 equal batches

160g granulated sugar

½ tsp orange food colouring

½ tsp orange flavouring


Orange Buttercream Filling

50 g butter

100 g icing sugar

2 tbsp milk

2 tbsp marmalade

2 drops of orange extract

Directions

1) 'Age' egg whites in fridge covered overnight

2) Place the icing sugar and ground almonds in the milling attachment of food processor and pulse about 15 times until fully combined. Sieve this mixture into a large bowl, discarding any particles that stay in the sieve. Add the first batch of egg whites to the almond mixture, mix to form a thick paste and set aside.

3) Tip the second batch of egg whites into a spotlessly clean, heatproof bowl and have an electric whisk at the ready.

4) Place 50ml water and the granulated sugar into a small saucepan on medium heat. Bring to a boil and cook until the syrup registers 110C, at which time start to beat the egg whites on high speed.

5) Once the syrup is at 118C pour it slowly down the side of the mixer bowl, avoiding the moving whisk. Continue to whisk on high until the mixture has cooled slightly and you have a shiny peaked meringue mixture – the bowl should no longer be hot to the touch, but still warm. Add the colouring and flavouring and whisk to combine.

6) Tip the meringue onto the almond mixture and gently fold together. It is important not to over-mix the batter – it should fall in a thick ribbon from the spatula. The ribbon should also fade back into the batter within about 30 secs – if it doesn’t, fold a few more times.

7) Heat oven to 150C. Line 3 baking sheets with baking parchment.

8) Transfer the batter to a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle. Pipe rounds about 2.5cm in diameter onto the prepared baking sheets. Leave to rest for 30 mins out of the fridge, or until the macaroons have developed a skin.

9) Bake the macaroons for 14 mins (this needs to be precise so you could test a macaroon first). Immediately slide the parchment onto the work surface and cool for a few minutes before gently peeling the macaroons off the paper.

10) Make buttercream filling by mixing together ingredients adding extra sugar if needed to get desired consistency.

11) Sandwich two macarons together with the filling
