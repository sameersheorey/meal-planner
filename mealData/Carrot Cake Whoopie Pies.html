Carrot Cake Whoopie Pies

Baking, Cakes

Prep Time: 40 mins Cook Time: 12-20 mins Total Time: 1 hour Servings: 24 Source: The Baking Club - BakedIn

Ingredients

For the cakes

115g butter

90g light brown sugar

80g caster sugar

1/2 tsp vanilla essence

2 medium eggs ( 1 whole + 1 yolk)

150g finely grated carrot (2-3 large)

190g plain flour

1.25 tsp ground cinnamon

0.75 tsp baking powder

0.5 tsp ground ginger


For the icing

100g unsalted butter

200g icing sugar

0.25 tsp ground cinnamon


For decorating

5g pistachios

40g white chocolate

Directions

1) Preheat oven to 160C fan/gas mark 4

2) Grease and line baking tray(s)

3) Melt 115g butter

4) Put light brown sugar, caster sugar and vanilla essence in a mixing bowl and add the melted butter. Mix until thoroughly combined

5) Add 1 whole egg and 1 egg yolk and mix until fully incorporated

6) Wash and peel carrots, then grate finely (total grated weight approx. 150g)

7) Put the grated carrot in a sieve and press out most of the moisture with the back of a spoon (total drained weight approx. 125g)

8) Fold the carrots into the mixture until they are completely coated

9) Add the flour, cinnamon, baking powder and ginger and mix until fully combined.

10) Spoon the mixture into a piping bags with large nozzle attached. Pipe flat rounds of mixture, no higher than 1cm, about the size of a 50p piece onto the baking tray. Leave a 3-4 cm gap between the cookies. (Make require multiple batches)

11) Bake for 12 minutes. Check that tops are set and edges are golden (bake for extra 3-8 minutes if needed)

12) Leave to cool on the baking tray for 5 minutes, the transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

13) While the cookies are cooling, make the buttercream. Beat 100g of softened butter in a bowl and gradually add the icing sugar and cinnamon.

14) Beat until thick and combined (if too thick too pipe loosen with a teaspoon of water)

15) To assemble, spoon the buttercream into piping bag with large nozzle. Pipe enough icing onto a cookie so that it spreads to the edge when sandwiched with a second cookie

16) Crush the pistachios to dust

17) Gentle melt the white chocolate and put in piping bag with thin (~1mm) nozzle. Drizzle over the whoopie pies. Before chocolate sets sprinkle over the crushed pistachio

Notes

Makes approx 48 cake cookies that sandwich together to make 24 whoopie pies.
May need to bake in multiple batches.
